Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this an official government service?

No, we are not a government service. We are a private company that assists individuals with completing the official National Consumer Commission (NCC) opt-out process. The opt-out itself is registered with the government, but we are an independent service provider that handles the paperwork on your behalf.

Why do I need to pay for this?

You don't have to pay — you can complete the opt-out process yourself for free through the NCC's official channels. Our R199 fee is for the convenience of having someone else handle the paperwork, document submission, and form completion for you. We're a time-saving service for those who prefer not to navigate the process themselves.

How long before calls stop?

There is no instant cutoff. Once your opt-out is registered with the NCC, telemarketers are legally required to update their systems and stop calling you. This process can take several weeks, and the exact timeframe depends on how often each company updates their contact lists. Different companies work at different speeds.

Why might I still receive some calls?

Even after opting out, you may still receive calls from: companies with whom you have an existing relationship, political organizations (exempt from opt-out regulations), market research surveys (also exempt), or companies operating illegally. The opt-out covers direct marketing calls, but not all types of calls are regulated under the same laws.

How is my personal information protected?

We use your information only to complete the NCC opt-out registration on your behalf. We do not sell, share, or use your information for marketing purposes. All documents are handled securely, and we comply with South Africa's Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA). Your information is stored only as long as necessary to complete the service.
